# Specification Document 4

# Reviewer Workflow Specification (RWS)

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Required Before Implementation
**Priority:** Critical

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines:

- Reviewer responsibilities
- Review lifecycle
- Approval criteria
- Rejection criteria
- Override behavior
- Publication workflow
- Review auditability

The reviewer is the final quality gate before content reaches learners.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

The AI system assists.

The reviewer decides.

---

## AI Responsibilities

```text
Retrieve Sources
Extract Knowledge
Resolve Conflicts
Generate Content
Calculate Confidence
```

---

## Reviewer Responsibilities

```text
Validate Accuracy
Validate Completeness
Validate Educational Quality
Approve Publication
```

---

# 3. Review Lifecycle

```text
Generated Content
        ↓
Pending Review
        ↓
Reviewer Evaluation
        ↓
 ┌─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┐
 │             │             │
 ▼             ▼             ▼
Approve      Reject      Regenerate
 │             │             │
 ▼             ▼             ▼
Publish      Archive     Reprocess
```

# 4. Review States

## Pending

```text
review_status = pending
```

---

## Approved

Content accepted.

```text
review_status = approved
```

---

## Rejected

Content unusable.

```text
review_status = rejected
```

---

## Needs Regeneration

Content partially correct but requires regeneration.

```text
review_status = needs_regeneration
```

# 6. Reviewer Checklist

Every topic must be evaluated against:

---

## Accuracy

Question:

```text
Is the content factually correct?
```

---

## Completeness

Question:

```text
Are important concepts missing?
```

---

## Source Alignment

Question:

```text
Does the generated content reflect the source material?
```

---

## Educational Quality

Question:

```text
Would a learner understand this topic?
```

---

## Consistency

Question:

```text
Are there contradictions?
```

---

# 7. Approval Criteria

Content may be approved when:

---

## Condition 1

No factual inaccuracies found.

---

## Condition 2

Critical fields populated.

Examples:

```text
Definition
Overview
Syntax
```

depending on topic type.

---

## Condition 3

Consistency check passed.

---

## Condition 4

Sources appear trustworthy.

---

## Condition 5

Educational quality acceptable.

---

# 8. Rejection Criteria

Reject when:

---

## Reason 1

Factually incorrect.

---

## Reason 2

Critical information missing.

---

## Reason 3

Content generated from weak sources.

---

## Reason 4

Contradictions unresolved.

---

## Reason 5

Content unusable without major rewriting.

---

# 9. Regeneration Criteria

Use regeneration when:

---

## Reason 1

Content mostly correct.

---

## Reason 2

Structure poor.

---

## Reason 3

Explanations unclear.

---

## Reason 4

Important sections weak.

---

Example:

```text
Definition correct
Benefits weak
Examples poor
```

↓

```text
Regenerate
```

instead of reject.

### Editorial Styling Guidelines
The reviewer workspace utilizes a high-contrast, double-border editorial design layout with Google Fonts (`Playfair Display` headers and `Outfit` body text), crisp sandstone borders, and warm peachy text selection highlighting (`rgba(173, 79, 48, 0.18)`). All interactive elements provide clear, visual keyboard focus visibility.

# 23. MVP Acceptance Criteria

The reviewer workflow is considered successful if:

1. Every generated topic is reviewable.
2. Every claim is traceable to sources.
3. Reviewers can inspect supporting evidence.
4. Review decisions are auditable.
5. Published content records reviewer identity.
6. Low-confidence content receives priority attention.
7. Overrides can be stored without modifying source-level knowledge.
